FAQ

Q1 : I'm stuck in crafting! Nothing left to craft and I'm not level 10!
A1 : You click on bars, converting stardust you get from brewing along with bars into EXP.
This is your main form of EXP for crafting.
Q2 : I have drills running, why am I not getting mining exp?! + What do mining levels do?!
A2 : Mining exp is gained by turning ore + stardust into EXP the same way you do in crafting.
By clicking ore and hitting “convert to xp”. This is the main way to get mining exp.

**What do mining levels do?**
They unlock the ability to use higher tier machines (when you craft them). Additionally once you gather
the tool “Chisel” your levels allow you to find higher tier geodes (Which are used for combat upgrades).

Q3 : Is Fishing or Finding seeds automatic?! Am I doing something wrong?
A3 : You fish and find seeds automatically. It's quite slow early game. For fishing, as you gain levels
and get more fishing tools this speeds up quite a lot. For finding seeds, its always quite slow but gathering
in the fields and upgrading your rake improves the seed rates substantially.
Q4 : Energy is so hard to get! How do I solve my energy problem???
A4 : You don't. Energy is a persistent problem through the entire game. That being said, in gathering you unlock
Fruit Tree fertilizer. This is a substantial upgrade to your energy income and is critical in getting. Additionally
upgrading your oven, socketing your fishing tools (so they get more exp and in turn catch more fish) and running
the “Fields” combat zone are all great ways to help mitigate your energy problems. Fields has 3 mobs who drop energy
when fought and is a very cheap way to combat when energy is scarce.
Q5 : How do I unlock Woodcutting!!!!!!!!!?
A5 : Try reading Progression section ...
deep deep down on this page : just next section ...

Progression

This section is just a Kick start as many people ask how to unlock basic stuff
like SKILLS ... More infos to be found in QUESTS section. Then game Chat !!

  1. Buy Pickaxe from game shop
    (almost all is good to buy in game shop, when you have the coins go for it)
  2. Do Dorins Quest [unlocks Crafting skill]
  3. craft all/anything you able to, from craft list
    (furnace will be the mother of your bars, eating your ores in the process..)
  4. Do Gem Seeker Quest
  5. sell some mining and/or crafting stuff to buy more in shop
  6. Buy Leather then craft Gloves [unlocks Gathering skill]
  7. Do Achievements Quest [unlocking Farming skill] in the process
  8. from now on seeds may be found at random pace and available inside farming tab
  9. during crafting process turn on/off machines when needs be : oil refill
  10. do eggplant quest to [unlcok Brewing skill]
  11. [unlock cooking skill] (by crafting the bronze oven)
  12. buy a weapon for next quest and to open combat MELEE
    (sell 500 stone and sell 250 coppers will match a weapon price btw)
  13. dont forget to click(equip) on the sword ... and try the fight
  14. this has triggered the next quest
  15. do the ent quest : kill it to [unlock Woodcutting skill]
  16. do Faradox's Introduction quest will [unlock Healing spell]
  17. fight in the fields to get enough string and craft a fishing net that will
    help launching the next quest : the fisherman ... doing it will [unlock Fishing skill]
  18. crafting the 1st community center will load the game shop with 3 new items:
    • arcade ticket 1000 gold (spam click rock for some money)
    • 3 coconuts (= energy) 8000 gold
    • a new tool for crafting : the hammer 5000 gold
  19. From Now on, Focus on QUESTS altogether with Achievements (=> highly recommended you
    read all and see if you cannot do some several achiev together ... might save time & stuff)

    Chat will be your friend to fill in the gaps when you need more than this page can provide.
    Have fun & remember : patience is your friend in this game ...

Basics :
Idle Pixel runs on a tick system.
You'll learn the rhythm of it the more you play.

There are 2 major portions to combat :
  • Your gear and stats
  • Your spells
The game starts very basically then grows more complex.
Until the “Volcano” combat area, the combat is very simple.
No micro-management or strategy used outside of “click & heal”.
(!)Kindly not pay too much attention to sets of bow-arrows-shield at once
Additionally in this game there are 3 types of mobs :
  • Common Mobs
  • [R]are mobs (~20% chance)
  • Shiny mobs (not available until a specific quest, chance to meet 1%)

Once EASY combat achievement is completed, every 10lv of combined melee/range
will add some permanent 1HP Max upgrade, and magic will do the same to mana.

The effective (but boring) "magic level training" is this :
sit in the FIELDS with a bow and **no-arrow** to be able to use the highest spells
availables as many times as possible before fight ends.
(Game wont let you enter a fight with no-arrow but you may build a preset without
OR exit fight to take off arrows)

Starting from Volcano, the dumb "hit-and-heal" combat process will switch to
using combat presets and try to fit the best action with best timing ...

Presets are Button binded equippement selections you made and saved.
Up to 5 presets available.
During a fight pressing a button will thus swap to desired preset

Fields :
  • As soon as you start learning magic try to use full
    Mana on every fight to keep building magic levels

Volcano :
** Ice arrows can potentially do nice damages to Volcano mobs **

  • Use Fight Presets :
    1. Melee : Sword + Shield
    2. Magic : Staff + shield
    3. Poison : Stinger + Shield
    4. Ranged : Bow + Shield + regular arrows

  • Monster strategy :
    1. Fire Snake : Use owned spells[heal/fire/reflect] and/OR Sword
      (watch out for his drop of the Fire spell to learn it)
    2. Fire Golem : Cast Fire (or Attack) as much as possible except when Golem
      is down to 1 Hp where a final Fire would blast his bomb.
      You dont want that to happen unless needed for a specific quest ...
      At 1 Hp better luck is use preset 3 : Poison Stinger
      Its very random and probably low win chance fight when starting volcano
      but later will become more doable.
    3. Fire Hawk : Cast Fire and (or) Attack as much as possible

    4. Fire Witch : use all but 1 Mana casting Fire, then melee Attack
      until shes very low on Hp and starts shaking : use Reflect to end her
      ** or accept your death for a specific quest ... **
Main Drops :
=> *IF* you own iron buckets : 1 per won fight will be filled with lava here
=> If you have completed a specific quest : 1 fight will also reward molten glass

Fire Snake : Fire-SPell !!
[use of Fire Spell is only possible when a staff is equipped]

Fire Golem : Bomb (used in mining tab : will potentialy reward every type of ore + 1 promethium Bar)
additionaly reward 5k mining Exp

Fire Hawk : Fire Feathers (used in crafting : Fire Arrow)
Fire Arrows do higher damage than normal ones, up to 5 (10 in northern Fields)
(will need a load of these arrows in the Norther Fields ...)

Fire Witch[R] :
String similar to fields spider's drop
Fire Crystal (one of the 4 story crystals)
Heart (Max Hp Upgrade) **unique item**

Northern Fields :
Preparation is key to have if any success in this area.

Delay your coming untill :
- high stats like : 25Hp, 13Mana ... best 14Mana Vs Yeti
- Are able to wear a FULL bear armor : If monster wont kill you, cold will
- Have a large stash of fire arrows (around/over 200 is a fair start)
- if Possible crafted some Rings (chisel & Geodes content)

Carpenter quest will help a lot for this area too

** Side Note : You will be requested 60 molten glass => to be included in preparation **
  • Use Fight Presets : Each with full bear armor
    1. Ranged : Bow (short until swap long one) + Shield + Fire Arrows
    2. Magic : Staff + Shield
    3. Ranged : Bow (short until swap long one) + Fire Arrows
      ** Yes same as #1 but without a shield see Ice Golem **
    4. Melee : Sword (Later replaced with Spiked Club) + Shield

  • Monster strategy :
    1. Ice Hawk :
      Fire arrows (and spell if needs be)
      Use reflect, when he shutters and shake, for a big damage back to him.

    2. Ice Golem :
      Spam fire on cooldown, do a naked reflect using preset #3
      (a no shield preset guarantee a hit thus guarantee a reflected damage)

    3. Ice Witch :
      Cast Fire twice, Reflect when she shakes at 1Hp
      Cast fire twice more ... and pray that all ends well ...

    4. Yeti :
      The Yeti has a freezing attack. He shakes, then freezes you for about… 6 seconds (~2 attacks)
      This attack can be reflected OR also broken by a fire spell.

      So the fight starts, you DON'T cast any spells. When he shakes, reflect the very 1st freeze.
      Still don't cast any spells. From now on when he is able to freeze you, just break out with fire
      Having the long bow and possibly accuracy ring(+2?) equipped will also make the fight easier.

    Main Drops :
    Ice Hawk : Ice Arrow (used in crafting : Ice Arrow)
    Ice Arrows base damage of 7 (.. are a nice treat for the Volcano Fire Golem)

    Ice Golem : Long Bow Frame (used in crafting : Long Bow)
    and arrows ... potentially a bunch of normal and/or fire, ice ... cool to replace/stockpile

    Fire Witch[R] : ** 2x uniques **
    Heart (Max Hp Upgrade)
    Magic Crystal (Max Mana Upgrade)

    Yeti :
    Club (used in crafting : spiked club upgrade) is both strong and slow,
    upgraded for a total of 12 damage and 3 accuracy
